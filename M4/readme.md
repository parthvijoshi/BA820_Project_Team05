## Parthvi Joshi -Summary 
Milestone 4 extends the previous analysis by validating and stress-testing the relationship between survival difficulty and audience engagement in Alone. Using hierarchical clustering, multi-seed stability checks, and PCA-based regression, the analysis confirms that difficulty is multidimensional and structurally stable across methods. Seasons characterized by sustained survival endurance and higher medical or environmental risk consistently receive stronger IMDb ratings but attract smaller average audiences. In contrast, more moderate seasons achieve broader reach but lower perceived quality. These results demonstrate a robust reach-versus-prestige trade-off embedded in the structure of survival difficulty, suggesting that producers must strategically balance audience size against brand intensity and long-term loyalty rather than assuming that harsher environments automatically maximize engagement.
